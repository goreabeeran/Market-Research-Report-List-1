<p><p>The Seawater Flue Gas Desulfurization (FGD) system market is categorized into three primary types: Wet FGD Systems utilize seawater to absorb and remove sulfur dioxide from flue gases, resulting in a stable effluent suitable for ocean discharge. Dry FGD Systems use dry reagents, such as lime, to adsorb sulfur dioxide, with minimal water usage and reduced waste. Semi-Dry FGD Systems combine features of both, using a spray dryer absorber to convert reagents into a dry powder for easier disposal and efficient SO2 capture.</p></p>
