<p><p>The Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) devices market primarily includes two types: Digital PCR (dPCR) and Real-time PCR (qPCR). Digital PCR allows for the absolute quantification of DNA by partitioning samples into numerous individual reactions, enhancing sensitivity and precision. In contrast, Real-time PCR enables the detection and quantification of DNA in real-time during the amplification process, utilizing fluorescent dyes. Both technologies are crucial for applications in medical diagnostics, research, and environmental monitoring, offering distinct advantages depending on the specific requirements.</p></p>
