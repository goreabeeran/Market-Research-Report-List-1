<p><p>The flat lithium-ion battery market is primarily divided into two types: liquified lithium-ion batteries and polymer lithium-ion batteries. Liquified lithium-ion batteries utilize a liquid electrolyte, offering high energy density and optimal thermal conductivity, making them suitable for larger applications like electric vehicles and energy storage systems. In contrast, polymer lithium-ion batteries employ a solid or gel-like polymer electrolyte, providing lightweight and flexible designs. This makes them ideal for compact devices such as smartphones, wearables, and portable electronics.</p></p>
