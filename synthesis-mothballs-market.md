<p><p>The synthesis mothballs market primarily consists of two types: naphthalene mothballs and paradichlorobenzene (PDB) mothballs. Naphthalene mothballs are made from naphthalene, a polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon, commonly used for moth control and odor prevention. In contrast, paradichlorobenzene mothballs utilize paradichlorobenzene, a chlorine-substituted aromatic compound, known for its strong antimicrobial properties. Both types serve similar purposes in pest repellent applications, but differ in chemical formulation, effectiveness, and environmental impact, catering to varying consumer preferences and regulatory standards.</p></p>
