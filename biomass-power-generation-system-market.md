<p><p>Biomass power generation systems are categorized into three main types. Direct combustion power generation involves burning biomass directly to produce steam for electricity. Mixed combustion combines biomass with fossil fuels to enhance energy output and efficiency. Gasification power generation converts biomass into synthesis gas through thermal processes, which can be used to generate electricity or produce biofuels. Each method offers unique advantages in efficiency, emissions, and resource use, catering to different energy needs and environmental goals.</p></p>
