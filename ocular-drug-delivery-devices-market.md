<p><p>The ocular drug delivery devices market comprises various types, each designed to enhance therapeutic efficacy. Ocular implants provide sustained drug release directly into the eye, while microneedles facilitate painless delivery through the eye's surface. Ocular inserts are solid or semi-solid devices placed in the conjunctival sac for prolonged release. Punctal plugs occlude tear ducts to retain medication. Viscosurgical devices are used during eye surgeries to maintain intraocular pressure and protect tissues. Other devices include novel technologies aimed at improving ocular drug delivery.</p></p>
