<p><p>Confocal Scanning Laser Ophthalmoscopy (CSLO) is an advanced imaging technique used in ophthalmology to obtain high-resolution images of the retina and anterior eye structures. By utilizing a laser to illuminate the retina and capturing the reflected light, CSLO provides detailed, cross-sectional images that enable healthcare professionals to diagnose and monitor various ocular conditions, including glaucoma, retinal diseases, and macular degeneration. </p><p>The market for Confocal Scanning Laser Ophthalmoscopy is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of eye disorders, advancements in laser imaging technology, and a growing aging population that is more susceptible to vision-related issues. <p><p>The confocal scanning laser ophthalmoscopy market is categorized into Wide Field and Ultra Wide Field types.
